## APPENDIX: WHY THIS SIMPLIFIED APPROACH WORKS

**Traditional stakeholder approval is for:**
- Large organizations with formal governance
- Projects with external funding/buy-in needed
- High-risk initiatives requiring sign-off

**Solo founder validation is for:**
- Personal projects where you're the decision maker
- Situations where you have domain expertise
- Need to move fast without bureaucracy

**You still benefit from:**
- Forcing yourself to validate assumptions
- Talking to real users before building
- Creating a decision checkpoint (avoid wasting months)
- Documenting your thinking

**The key difference:**
- 1-2 days vs 1-2 weeks
- Informal vs formal
- Self-approval vs external sign-off
- "Good enough" validation vs exhaustive research

**This approach balances:**
- ‚úÖ Speed (so you can start building)
- ‚úÖ Rigor (so you're not building blindly)
- ‚úÖ Pragmatism (appropriate for solo founder context)
